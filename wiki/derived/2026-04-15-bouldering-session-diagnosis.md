# Bouldering Session Weakness Diagnosis — 2026-04-15

> **TL;DR**: Week-1 Moonboard 40° session attempting two V7s (`LAST CALL`,
> `LAPUTA`) surfaced four specific limiters for areomoon: power endurance,
> left-side lock-off asymmetry, off-axial grip weakness, and bicep-dominant
> compression pulling. None of these are pull-strength bottlenecks — consistent
> with V5→V7 transition expectations from
> [Climbing Training System](../concepts/climbing-training-system.md).

## Key Points

- **续航 (PE) gap**: moves individually clean, link-ups fail → classic
  power-endurance deficit, not max-strength deficit. See
  [Climbing Power Endurance](../concepts/climbing-power-endurance.md).
- **Left-side lock-off asymmetry**: unique finding — the weaker side is
  load-bearing on `LAST CALL` (all-left hold sequence, 7A+/V7, Moonboard 40°).
  Unilateral programming required. See
  [Climbing Lock-Off Strength](../concepts/climbing-lock-off-strength.md).
- **Off-axial grip failure**: "totally cannot grip" certain Moonboard holds
  where the force vector is not straight down. Indicates under-developed
  specificity on sloper, pinch, and gaston angles — already a known V6-V7
  limiter in the training system article.
- **Compression pull deficit**: `LAST CALL` final sequence fails on
  bicep-driven inward pull despite 20-rep pull-up baseline. Pulls are not
  compressions — they use different muscles. See
  [Climbing Compression Pulling](../concepts/climbing-compression-pulling.md).
