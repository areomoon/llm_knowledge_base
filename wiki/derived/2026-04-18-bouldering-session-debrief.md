# Bouldering Session Debrief — 2026-04-17 (Fri Gym Day)

> **TL;DR**: Friday gym session executed the asymmetric-pull + lock-off
> prescription derived from the 2026-04-15 Moonboard diagnosis. Main block
> (B1–B4) landed at high quality; tail was trimmed (C reduced, D skipped)
> by fasted-state fatigue. Self-scored 8.5/10. Two cross-session signals
> now point at the **bicep / elbow-flexor chain** as the *dosage* constraint
> — not the exercise-selection question — and at pre-session **fueling** as
> a non-negotiable for days where Core is last in the plan.

## Key Points

- **Plan executed B-block in full, trimmed tail by design.** B1 offset
  pull-up, B2 90° pause pull, B3 single-arm lock-off, B4 typewriter all
  completed. C1 (narrow supinated chin-up) reduced in reps; C2 pullover
  controlled; C3 pinch adjusted for slip/weight; C4 supinated curl at
  lower intensity. D (core) dropped after hunger + fatigue — non-essential
  call was correct.

- **Cross-session bicep/brachialis signal.** Typewriter set 1 at near-limit
  indicates the B1–B3 sequence had already drained the shared elbow-flexor
  pool. Combined with the 04-15 `LAST CALL` compression failure — two
  independent sessions now flag the bicep chain. See
  [Climbing Compression Pulling](../concepts/climbing-compression-pulling.md).
  **Interpretation**: exercise selection is correct; dosage and ordering
  are now the knob.

- **Energy state as a first-class variable.** Fasted + coffee → post-B
  hunger → C reduced, D dropped. Next Fri gym: carbs 90–120 min
  pre-session. User's own synthesis — not drawn from a named authority —
  but consistent with general glycogen-depletion literature for 90-min
  resistance sessions.

- **Rest discipline preserved force quality at the cost of wall-clock time.**
  2–3 min inter-set, 5–6 min inter-exercise ran the session to ~90 min.
  Correct regime for the B block (the unilateral quality target is the
  point), but it compounded the fueling gap into the tail. See
  [Climbing Lock-Off Strength](../concepts/climbing-lock-off-strength.md)
  on why force quality on unilateral lock-off drills is sensitive to
  inter-set recovery.
